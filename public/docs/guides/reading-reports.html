Reading and Understanding Your Reports

The Reports section provides comprehensive insights into your nutrition and weight tracking data. This guide will help you understand and interpret the various charts and statistics available.

Accessing Reports

Navigate to the Reports page by clicking "Reports" in the main navigation menu. The reports page displays various charts and analytics based on your food diary and weight tracking data.

Types of Reports Available

Nutrition Overview

This section provides a high-level summary of your nutritional intake:

  • Daily Calorie Average: Your average daily calorie consumption
  • Macronutrient Distribution: Breakdown of proteins, carbs, and fats
  • Goal Progress: How you're tracking against your nutrition goals
  • Consistency Score: How regularly you're logging your meals

Weight Progress Charts

Visual representations of your weight journey:

  • Weight Trend Line: Shows your weight changes over time
  • Moving Averages: Smoothed trend lines to reduce daily fluctuation noise
  • Goal Progress: Visual progress toward your weight goals
  • Rate of Change: How quickly you're gaining or losing weight

Understanding Nutrition Charts

Daily Calorie Intake Chart

This line chart shows your daily calorie consumption over time:

  • Green line: Your daily calorie goal
  • Blue bars: Your actual daily intake
  • Red zone: Days significantly over your goal
  • Yellow zone: Days slightly over your goal

What to Look For:

  • Consistency in staying near your goal line
  • Patterns in overeating (weekends, stress periods)
  • Days with very low intake that might need attention

Macronutrient Breakdown

Pie charts and bar graphs showing your protein, carbohydrate, and fat intake:

  • Percentage distribution: What portion of calories comes from each macro
  • Gram amounts: Actual quantities consumed
  • Goal comparison: How your intake compares to recommended ratios

Healthy Ranges:

  • Protein: 10-35% of total calories
  • Carbohydrates: 45-65% of total calories
  • Fats: 20-35% of total calories

Micronutrient Analysis

Charts showing your vitamin and mineral intake:

  • Daily values: Percentage of recommended daily intake
  • Deficiency alerts: Nutrients you might be lacking
  • Excess warnings: Nutrients you might be consuming too much of

Interpreting Weight Charts

Weight Trend Analysis

The main weight chart displays several important elements:

  • Raw data points: Your actual daily weight measurements
  • 7-day moving average: Smoothed weekly trend
  • 30-day moving average: Long-term trend direction
  • Goal line: Your target weight (if set)

Reading the Trends:

  • Upward slope: Weight gain trend
  • Downward slope: Weight loss trend
  • Flat line: Weight maintenance
  • Zigzag pattern: Normal daily fluctuations

Rate of Change Chart

This chart shows how quickly your weight is changing:

  • Weekly rate: Average pounds/kg lost or gained per week
  • Monthly rate: Longer-term rate of change
  • Healthy ranges: Recommended rates for sustainable progress

Healthy Weight Loss Rates:

  • Sustainable: 1-2 pounds (0.5-1 kg) per week
  • Aggressive: 2-3 pounds (1-1.5 kg) per week (short-term only)
  • Too fast: More than 3 pounds (1.5 kg) per week

Time Period Filters

Adjusting Date Ranges

Use the date range selectors to focus on specific periods:

  • Last 7 days: Recent short-term trends
  • Last 30 days: Monthly patterns and progress
  • Last 90 days: Quarterly trends and seasonal patterns
  • Last year: Long-term progress and annual patterns
  • Custom range: Specific periods you want to analyze

Comparing Different Periods

Some reports allow you to compare different time periods:

  • This month vs. last month
  • Current quarter vs. previous quarter
  • Year-over-year comparisons

Identifying Patterns and Insights

Weekly Patterns

Look for recurring weekly patterns in your data:

  • Weekend eating: Higher calorie intake on weekends
  • Weekday consistency: More structured eating during work days
  • Social eating: Impact of social events on your nutrition

Seasonal Trends

Identify how seasons affect your eating and weight:

  • Holiday periods: Weight gain during celebrations
  • Summer vs. winter: Activity level and food choice changes
  • Stress periods: How life events impact your habits

Correlation Analysis

Look for relationships between different metrics:

  • High-calorie days and weight changes
  • Protein intake and satiety/hunger levels
  • Exercise days and nutrition choices
  • Sleep quality and eating patterns

Using Reports for Goal Setting

Setting Realistic Targets

Use your historical data to set achievable goals:

  • Base calorie goals on your actual average intake
  • Set weight loss rates based on your past success
  • Identify your most successful patterns to replicate

Tracking Progress

Regular report review helps you:

  • Celebrate achievements and milestones
  • Identify areas needing improvement
  • Adjust goals based on real results
  • Stay motivated with visual progress

Exporting and Sharing Data

Export Options

Most reports can be exported for external use:

  • PDF reports: Professional-looking summaries
  • CSV data: Raw data for spreadsheet analysis
  • Image files: Charts for sharing or printing

Sharing with Healthcare Providers

Your reports can be valuable for:

  • Nutritionist consultations
  • Doctor visits and health assessments
  • Personal trainer sessions
  • Insurance wellness programs

Troubleshooting Report Issues

Missing Data

If reports seem incomplete:

  • Check that you've logged meals consistently
  • Verify your date range selections
  • Ensure you have enough data for meaningful analysis

Unexpected Results

If numbers don't match your expectations:

  • Review your food logging accuracy
  • Check for duplicate entries
  • Verify portion sizes and measurements
  • Consider factors not tracked in the app
