Tracking Your Weight Progress

Weight tracking is an essential part of monitoring your health and fitness journey. This guide will show you how to effectively use the weight tracking features in Food Diary.

Getting Started with Weight Tracking

Navigate to the main dashboard or diary page where you'll find the weight tracking section. This area displays your current weight statistics and provides options to add new weight entries.

Adding Weight Entries

Recording Your Weight

  1. Locate the weight tracking section on your dashboard
  2. Click the "Add Weight" or "+" button
  3. Enter your current weight in your preferred unit (lbs or kg)
  4. The current date will be automatically selected, but you can change it if needed
  5. Click "Save" to record your weight entry

Best Times to Weigh Yourself

For the most consistent and accurate tracking:

  • Same time daily: Weigh yourself at the same time each day
  • Morning is best: First thing in the morning, after using the bathroom
  • Before eating: Before breakfast and drinking fluids
  • Minimal clothing: Wear the same amount of clothing each time
  • Same scale: Use the same scale in the same location

Understanding Weight Statistics

The weight tracking section displays several key metrics:

Today's Weight

Your most recent weight measurement. If you haven't weighed yourself today, this shows your last recorded weight with the date.

Last Change

The difference between your current weight and your previous weight entry. This shows:

  • Positive numbers indicate weight gain
  • Negative numbers indicate weight loss
  • The time period between the two measurements

Overall Change

The total weight change since you started tracking. This is calculated from your first weight entry to your most recent one.

Average Change

The average change between consecutive weight measurements. This helps smooth out daily fluctuations and shows your overall trend.

Progress Indicator

A visual indicator showing whether your weight is:

  • Trending Down: Consistent weight loss
  • Stable: Maintaining current weight
  • Trending Up: Consistent weight gain

Managing Weight Entries

Editing Previous Entries

To modify a previous weight entry:

  1. Navigate to the weight history or click "View History"
  2. Find the entry you want to edit
  3. Click the edit icon next to the entry
  4. Update the weight value or date
  5. Save your changes

Deleting Incorrect Entries

To remove an incorrect weight entry:

  1. Locate the entry in your weight history
  2. Click the delete/trash icon
  3. Confirm the deletion when prompted

Setting Weight Goals

Defining Your Target

To set up weight goals:

  1. Go to the Settings page
  2. Find the "Weight Goals" section
  3. Enter your target weight
  4. Set your desired timeline (optional)
  5. Choose your goal type (lose, gain, or maintain)

Tracking Progress Toward Goals

Once you've set a goal, the system will:

  • Show your progress percentage
  • Calculate remaining weight to reach your goal
  • Estimate timeline based on current trends
  • Provide motivation and milestone celebrations

Understanding Weight Fluctuations

Normal Daily Variations

It's normal for weight to fluctuate daily due to:

  • Water retention: Affected by sodium intake, hormones, and exercise
  • Food timing: When you last ate and how much
  • Bathroom habits: Regularity affects daily weight
  • Sleep patterns: Poor sleep can affect water retention
  • Stress levels: Stress hormones can impact weight

Focus on Trends, Not Daily Changes

Instead of worrying about daily fluctuations:

  • Look at weekly averages
  • Focus on the overall trend direction
  • Consider 7-day and 30-day moving averages
  • Celebrate consistent progress over time

Using Weight Data with Nutrition Tracking

Correlating Weight with Food Intake

Combine your weight tracking with food diary data to:

  • Identify which eating patterns support your goals
  • Understand how different foods affect your weight
  • Adjust calorie intake based on weight trends
  • Find the right balance for sustainable progress

Making Data-Driven Adjustments

Use your combined data to:

  • Increase calories if losing weight too quickly
  • Decrease calories if not seeing expected progress
  • Adjust macronutrient ratios based on results
  • Time meals around exercise for better results

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Weight Not Changing

If your weight seems stuck:

  • Review your food logging accuracy
  • Check for hidden calories (oils, condiments, drinks)
  • Consider increasing physical activity
  • Be patient - plateaus are normal

Unexpected Weight Gain

If you see sudden weight increases:

  • Check for high sodium intake the previous day
  • Consider hormonal factors (menstrual cycle, stress)
  • Review recent exercise intensity (muscle inflammation)
  • Don't panic - wait a few days to see the trend

Privacy and Data Security

Your weight data is:

  • Stored securely and privately
  • Never shared without your explicit consent
  • Backed up to prevent data loss
  • Exportable if you want to use other tools
