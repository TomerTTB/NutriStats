Weight Tracking

The Weight Tracking feature helps you monitor your weight changes over time, providing detailed statistics and visual representations of your progress.

Key Statistics

Weight Metrics

  • Today's Weight:
    • Your most recent weight measurement
    • Displayed in your chosen unit system (kg/lbs)
    • Updated with each new entry
  • Last Change:
    • Difference between your last two measurements
    • Shows short-term progress
    • Includes direction indicator (gain/loss)
  • Overall Change:
    • Total weight change since starting
    • Compares first and latest measurements
    • Shows long-term progress
  • Average Change:
    • Average change between measurements
    • Indicates typical rate of change
    • Helps identify trends
  • Progress:
    • Overall trend direction
    • Visual indicator (increasing/decreasing)
    • Based on recent measurements

Weight Entry

Adding Weight Measurements

  1. Navigate to the Weight Tracking section
  2. Enter your current weight
  3. Select the date (defaults to today)
  4. Add optional notes
  5. Click "Save" to record the entry

Managing Entries

  • Editing Entries:
    • Click the edit icon on any entry
    • Update weight or date
    • Modify or add notes
    • Save changes
  • Deleting Entries:
    • Click the delete icon
    • Confirm deletion
    • Statistics update automatically

Visual Tracking

Weight Chart

  • Data Points:
    • Each weight entry shown on timeline
    • Interactive hover information
    • Click for detailed view
  • Trend Line:
    • Shows overall progress direction
    • Smooths daily fluctuations
    • Helps identify patterns

Best Practices

Measurement Tips

  • Weigh yourself at the same time each day
  • Use consistent conditions (clothing, time of day)
  • Record weight regularly for better tracking
  • Note any factors affecting weight in the notes
  • Don't focus too much on daily fluctuations

Understanding Changes

  • Normal weight can fluctuate 1-2 kg daily
  • Look for overall trends rather than daily changes
  • Consider factors like:
    • Water retention
    • Time of day
    • Recent meals
    • Exercise
